The problem

Exams have one fatal design flaw

One paper. For every student.

In 2024, India's NEET medical entrance exam was leaked — reportedly ₹1.5 crore per paper, multiple arrests. The moment one copy escaped, the entire exam was compromised.

The industry's answer? More surveillance. Webcams, eye-tracking, AI proctoring.

⚠️ AI proctoring is biased against neurodivergent & disabled students, and is being restricted by courts and data-protection law. It doesn't even stop a photo taken six hours before the exam.
The insight

Don't watch the student. Break the paper.

Every exam ever made shares the same flaw: a single paper for everyone. So we removed it.

Maya-Exana gives every student a mathematically unique paper generated from one blueprint. A leaked answer key fits no one else.

There is nothing worth sharing — because the answers you copied are wrong by design.

The seed is the exam. The same studentID always rebuilds the exact same paper (auditable & verifiable offline), yet every student's paper differs (uncheatable). No question string is ever stored on a server, so there is nothing to leak.
Closed loop for paper exams: a scanned OMR sheet (just an ID + bubbled letters) is graded by regenerating that exact paper from its seed — no answer key needs to be stored or shipped.

Engineering integrity

This is not an AI wrapper

The trust-critical path — generation, grading, psychometrics — makes zero API calls. It's verifiable, deterministic math you can audit offline in ten minutes.

  • Pure, deterministic JavaScript · zero runtime dependencies
  • A grade is reproducible & explainable — never a non-deterministic LLM guess
  • AI is optional authoring assistance only, fully separated from grading
Documented as a deliberate architecture decision in DESIGN.md (ADR-001).
Ethics by construction

Integrity without surveillance

What we DON'T do

No webcam. No face or eye tracking. No biometrics. No black-box accusations.

What we DO

Consented behavioural signals — timing, paste events, focus loss — each with a plain-English reason. A human examiner decides.

Maya-Exana never accuses. It surfaces explainable signals — legally and ethically defensible where opaque AI proctoring is being banned.

The one-line difference: legacy tools pull random questions from a server database. Maya-Exana derives every paper from a cryptographically auditable seed — no question is ever stored to leak, and any paper is verifiable offline.

Parameterized generation itself isn't new (Khan, WeBWorK, MyOpenMath). The contribution is the combination: treating determinism as the anti-cheat primitive, closing the loop to physical paper via seed-regeneration (gradeFromOMR), and auto-flagging broken items via CTT.
